Education

Ph.D. in Control Science and Engineering

June 2025
Jiangnan University, Wuxi, Jiangsu Province, P.R. China

My doctoral research focused on control science and engineering, with a particular emphasis on system modeling and identification, especially continuous-time systems. My dissertation, "Parameter Identification of Continuous-Time Systems Based on Laplace Transform and Differential Approximation," introduces an efficient and accurate method for estimating dynamic system parameters. This work is closely related to electrical engineering and automation, supporting precise control in electric drives, power systems, and industrial automation. By combining control theory with practical engineering applications, my research has advanced the development of intelligent adaptive control systems.

Master's Degree in Electrical Engineering

July 2018
Jiangnan University, Wuxi, Jiangsu Province, P.R. China

During my Master's studies in Electrical Engineering and Automation, my research focused on motor drives, particularly sensorless vector control of induction motors. My dissertation was titled "Research on Sensorless Vector Control of Induction Motors Based on Model Reference Adaptive System (MRAS) Technology". This Thesis explores how to estimate rotor speed without relying on physical sensors using a Model Reference Adaptive System (MRAS). This approach improves the reliability of motor drive systems, reduces costs, and enhances performance. The primary application area for this work is industrial automation, where precise motor control is crucial, especially in environments where sensor installation is difficult or impractical, such as electric vehicles, robots, and automated manufacturing systems.
